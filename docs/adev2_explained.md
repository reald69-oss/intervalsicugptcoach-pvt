# 🧠 Scientific Alignment (ADE v2 — Complete)

ADE v2 is not a new theory. It is a **decision-layer synthesis** of established endurance science.

It integrates four domains:

---

# 1. Load & Fatigue Modelling (Primary Control Layer)

### Banister impulse-response model

* Defines **fitness vs fatigue dual response**
* ATL (acute load) rises faster than CTL (chronic adaptation)
* TSB represents current readiness

**Relevance to ADE v2:**

* Phase detection is fundamentally a **fatigue dominance check**
* Recovery phase is triggered when fatigue signal outweighs adaptive capacity

---

### Training monotony

### Training strain

* Monotony = lack of variation
* Strain = monotony × load

**Relevance to ADE v2:**

* These act as **risk amplifiers**
* Even “acceptable load” becomes unsafe when:

  * monotony is high
  * intensity clustering occurs

→ ADE v2 interprets this as **hidden fatigue accumulation**

---

# 2. Periodisation & Phase Governance (Override Layer)

### Block periodization

* Training must occur in **sequenced blocks**
* Each block has:

  * a dominant stimulus
  * a termination condition

**Critical point:**

> Blocks must end when adaptive saturation or fatigue accumulation is reached

**Relevance to ADE v2:**

* `required_phase` is effectively:
  → **block termination signal**

---

### Polarized training model

* Distinguishes:

  * microcycle load (weekly execution)
  * mesocycle structure (phase intent)

**Relevance to ADE v2:**

* ADE operates at **micro level (execution)**
* Phase operates at **meso level (strategy)**

→ ADE v2 enforces:

```text
mesocycle intent > microcycle optimisation
```

---

### Tapering in endurance training

* Performance improves when:

  * load is reduced
  * intensity is preserved
  * fatigue dissipates

**Critical finding:**

> Continued load during fatigue suppresses performance gains

**Relevance to ADE v2:**

* Recovery phase is not “rest”
* It is a **necessary performance mechanism**

---

# 3. Energy System & Adaptation Constraints

### Energy system development

* Aerobic, threshold, VO₂, anaerobic systems adapt at different rates

**Constraint:**

> All systems require recovery to consolidate adaptations

**Relevance to ADE v2:**

* ADE may detect:

  * VO₂ decline → suggest intensity
* BUT if fatigue present:

  * system blocks progression

→ prevents **false optimisation**

---

# 4. Failure Prevention (System Safety Layer)

### Overtraining syndrome

* Caused by:

  * sustained overload
  * insufficient recovery
  * monotony + intensity stacking

**Relevance to ADE v2:**

* Phase override exists specifically to prevent:

  * non-functional overreach
  * chronic maladaptation
